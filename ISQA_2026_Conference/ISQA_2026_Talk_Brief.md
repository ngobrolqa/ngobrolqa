## Abstract

QA professionals today are learning more than ever—automation, tools, and now AI. Yet many still find themselves stuck, not because they lack effort, but because they lack clarity.

This talk challenges a common assumption: growth in QA is not about learning more, but about learning what matters next.

Aditya Mirza Bahari introduces a stage-based perspective on QA skill development, showing how the challenges—and therefore the right focus—change at each level. Instead of following trends blindly, QA professionals need to understand their current level and identify the real bottleneck that is holding them back.

In the context of the AI era, this becomes even more critical. AI does not automatically improve your capabilities—it amplifies your current level. Without clarity, AI can accelerate the wrong direction.

Through a practical framework and an interactive self-assessment approach, this session will help you identify where you stand, understand your real problem, and make better decisions about what to learn next.

---

## Key Takeaways

Attendees will learn:

1. **Growth ≠ Learning More** - Why learning everything doesn't lead to growth; the importance of learning what matters *next*
2. **Stage-Based Challenges** - How the real bottleneck changes at each skill level, and why one-size-fits-all advice fails
3. **AI Amplifies Your Level** - AI doesn't automatically help—it accelerates wherever you already are, including the wrong direction
4. **Clarity Before Action** - A practical framework and self-assessment to identify where you stand and what to focus on
