WHERE AM I?

The Science of Skill Acquisition

🧠 What is the Dreyfus Model?

📚 The Origin Story

  • 1980: Stuart & Hubert Dreyfus (UC Berkeley)
  • Studied airline pilots, chess masters, nurses
  • Asked: "How do humans acquire skills?"
  • Found: 5 distinct stages of skill development

💡 Key Insight

Skill development is NOT linear.

It's about moving from:
📋 Following rules
→ ✨ Intuitive expertise

Used in nursing, aviation, military, software development... and now QA!

🌐 ngobrolqa.com ISQA Conference 2026

📊 The 5 Dreyfus Levels

How humans develop expertise (Dreyfus, 1980)

5
EXPERT
Intuitive decision-making, innovates new approaches
4
PROFICIENT
Sees the big picture, prioritizes effectively
3
COMPETENT
Plans systematically, handles complexity
2
ADV BEGINNER
Recognizes patterns from experience
1
NOVICE
Follows rules and instructions

🎯 Dreyfus in QA

NOVICE "I follow the test case step by step"
ADV BEGINNER "I can write test cases for my feature"
COMPETENT "I own quality E2E, coordinate with devs"
PROFICIENT "I mentor others, design test strategies"
EXPERT "I define standards, consult on critical decisions"

💡 You can be Level 4 in one skill, Level 2 in another!

📝 Real QA Examples by Level

Level 1-2: Bug Reporting

L1: "Found bug, app crashed"

L2: "App crashes when clicking submit with empty form"

Level 3-4: Bug Reporting

L3: Includes root cause, affected areas, workaround

L4: Proposes fix, estimates impact, prevents recurrence

Level 1-2: Test Planning

L1: Waits for test plan from lead

L2: Can estimate own testing effort

Level 3-4: Test Planning

L3: Creates risk-based test strategy

L4: Designs org-wide test frameworks

⚠️ When NOT to Trust AI

❌ DON'T

  • ⛔ Blindly copy AI-generated test cases
  • ⛔ Trust AI for security decisions
  • ⛔ Let AI replace domain knowledge
  • ⛔ Skip verification of outputs

✅ DO

  • ✓ Use AI output as a DRAFT
  • ✓ Verify against requirements
  • ✓ Apply YOUR expertise to refine
  • ✓ Question AI's assumptions

"AI is your copilot, not your autopilot"
