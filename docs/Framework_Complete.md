# PART 1: THE 5 STAGES

## Stage 1: STARTER
**Dreyfus Equivalent:** Novice

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Core Problem** | "I don't know what I don't know" |
| **What Holds Them Back** | Lack of foundation — no mental model of testing |
| **Next Move** | Learn fundamentals: what is testing, why it matters, basic techniques |
| **AI Role** | **AI as Teacher** — explain concepts, answer questions, provide examples |
| **Typical Profile** | Fresh graduate, career switcher, intern |
| **Time at Stage** | 0-1 year |

**Signs you're a Starter:**
- You follow instructions without understanding why
- You need checklists for everything
- You're not sure if your test is "good enough"
- You ask "what should I test?" frequently

**What Starters should NOT do:**
- Jump to automation without manual fundamentals
- Chase certifications without understanding basics
- Compare yourself to seniors

---

## Stage 2: PRACTITIONER
**Dreyfus Equivalent:** Advanced Beginner

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Core Problem** | "I can do it, but I'm not sure if it's right" |
| **What Holds Them Back** | Lack of confidence — can execute but doubts judgment |
| **Next Move** | Build patterns: practice techniques, get feedback, develop intuition |
| **AI Role** | **AI as Assistant** — draft test cases, explain bugs, suggest approaches |
| **Typical Profile** | Junior QA with 1-2 years experience |
| **Time at Stage** | 1-3 years |

**Signs you're a Practitioner:**
- You can write test cases independently
- You recognize common bug patterns
- You still ask seniors "does this look right?"
- You're faster at familiar tasks, slower at new ones

**What Practitioners should NOT do:**
- Stay in comfort zone of repetitive testing
- Avoid technical challenges (API, automation)
- Wait for direction instead of proposing

---

## Stage 3: PROFESSIONAL
**Dreyfus Equivalent:** Competent

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Core Problem** | "I'm good at my job, but I feel stuck" |
| **What Holds Them Back** | Lack of direction — competent but no differentiator |
| **Next Move** | Find your edge: specialize, build expertise, own something |
| **AI Role** | **AI as Accelerator** — automate routine work, focus on judgment calls |
| **Typical Profile** | Mid-level QA, 3-5 years experience |
| **Time at Stage** | 2-4 years |

**Signs you're a Professional:**
- You can own a feature end-to-end
- People trust your quality judgment
- You feel like "just another QA" — no unique value
- You're wondering "what's next for me?"

**What Professionals should NOT do:**
- Stay generalist forever — pick a direction
- Ignore soft skills — technical alone won't differentiate
- Wait to be promoted — create your own opportunities

---

## Stage 4: LEADER
**Dreyfus Equivalent:** Proficient

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Core Problem** | "I can lead, but my impact is limited" |
| **What Holds Them Back** | Lack of scale — personal impact doesn't multiply |
| **Next Move** | Multiply impact: mentor, build systems, influence beyond your team |
| **AI Role** | **AI as Multiplier** — scale practices, automate coaching, amplify reach |
| **Typical Profile** | Senior QA, Tech Lead, QA Lead, 5-8 years |
| **Time at Stage** | 3-5 years |

**Signs you're a Leader:**
- Juniors come to you for advice
- You see problems before they happen
- Your influence stops at your team boundary
- You want bigger impact but don't know how

**What Leaders should NOT do:**
- Hoard knowledge — your growth comes from growing others
- Stay purely technical — leadership requires influence
- Ignore organizational dynamics

---

## Stage 5: INDUSTRY
**Dreyfus Equivalent:** Expert

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Core Problem** | "How do I stay relevant and continue to grow?" |
| **What Holds Them Back** | Risk of stagnation — at the top, where to go? |
| **Next Move** | Shape the future: innovate, contribute to community, build legacy |
| **AI Role** | **AI as Partner** — co-create new approaches, explore frontiers together |
| **Typical Profile** | QA Manager, Principal QA, Consultant, 8+ years |
| **Time at Stage** | Ongoing |

**Signs you're Industry level:**
- You're known beyond your company
- People seek your opinion on QA trends
- You shape how others think about quality
- You contribute to the field (talks, writing, standards)

**What Industry leaders should NOT do:**
- Rest on reputation — the field keeps evolving
- Dismiss new approaches (like AI) — stay curious
- Forget where you came from — mentor the next generation

# PART 2: THE 4 DIMENSIONS

## Dimension 1: 🎯 TESTING FUNDAMENTALS
**Core Question:** How well do you practice the craft of testing?

### Sub-Areas

| Sub-Area | What It Covers |
|----------|---------------|
| **Test Design** | Creating test cases, applying techniques (BVA, EP, decision tables) |
| **Test Execution** | Running tests, documenting results, managing test data |
| **Defect Management** | Finding, reporting, tracking, analyzing bugs |
| **Test Analysis** | Understanding requirements, identifying what to test |

### Key Indicators by Stage

| Stage | Key Indicator |
|-------|---------------|
| **Starter** | I can execute test cases written by others and report basic bugs |
| **Practitioner** | I can write test cases independently using basic techniques |
| **Professional** | I can design test strategies for a feature using multiple techniques |
| **Leader** | I can define test approaches that others follow across projects |
| **Industry** | I can innovate testing methods that influence how others think about testing |

### ISTQB Alignment
- CTFL (Foundation Level) — covers all sub-areas
- CTAL-TA (Test Analyst) — advanced test design

---

## Dimension 2: ⚙️ TECHNICAL DEPTH
**Core Question:** How deep is your technical toolkit?

### Sub-Areas

| Sub-Area | What It Covers |
|----------|---------------|
| **Automation** | Test automation design, frameworks, CI/CD integration |
| **API & Integration** | API testing, service testing, integration points |
| **Performance** | Load testing, performance analysis, bottleneck identification |
| **Security Basics** | Security testing fundamentals, OWASP awareness |
| **AI-Assisted Testing** | Using AI to help you test (prompts, generation, analysis) |
| **AI System Testing** | Testing AI/ML products (hallucination, bias, accuracy) |

### Key Indicators by Stage

| Stage | Key Indicator |
|-------|---------------|
| **Starter** | I can use testing tools (Postman, browser devtools) at basic level |
| **Practitioner** | I can write simple automated tests and basic API tests |
| **Professional** | I can build automation frameworks and design API test suites |
| **Leader** | I can architect test infrastructure and set technical standards |
| **Industry** | I can evaluate and adopt emerging technologies (AI, new tools) for the organization |

### AI Skills Breakdown

**AI-Assisted Testing (CT-GenAI aligned):**

| Stage | AI-Assisted Testing |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Starter** | I can use AI to explain testing concepts and answer questions |
| **Practitioner** | I can use AI to draft test cases and suggest edge cases |
| **Professional** | I can craft effective prompts and validate AI outputs critically |
| **Leader** | I can integrate AI tools into team workflows and coach others |
| **Industry** | I can evaluate AI tools strategically and shape adoption roadmaps |

**AI System Testing (CT-AI aligned):**

| Stage | AI System Testing |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Starter** | I understand that AI systems need different testing approaches |
| **Practitioner** | I can identify basic AI risks (hallucination, bias) in products |
| **Professional** | I can design test cases for AI features (accuracy, consistency) |
| **Leader** | I can define AI testing strategies for products with AI components |
| **Industry** | I can establish AI quality standards and risk frameworks |

### ISTQB Alignment
- CT-TAE (Test Automation Engineer) — automation
- CT-PT (Performance Testing) — performance
- CT-SEC (Security Testing) — security
- CT-AI (AI Testing) — testing OF AI systems
- CT-GenAI (GenAI Testing) — testing WITH AI

## Dimension 3: 🧠 QUALITY THINKING
**Core Question:** How strategically do you think about quality?

### Sub-Areas

| Sub-Area | What It Covers |
|----------|---------------|
| **Risk-Based Thinking** | Identifying, assessing, prioritizing risks |
| **Root Cause Analysis** | Finding why defects happen, not just what |
| **Prevention Mindset** | Shifting left, quality gates, early feedback |
| **Test Strategy** | Planning approach based on context, not templates |
| **Metrics & Analysis** | Using data to drive quality decisions |

### Key Indicators by Stage

| Stage | Key Indicator |
|-------|---------------|
| **Starter** | I can identify obvious risks when pointed out |
| **Practitioner** | I can spot risks in requirements and flag them proactively |
| **Professional** | I can prioritize testing based on risk and business impact |
| **Leader** | I can define risk-based strategies that balance coverage and speed |
| **Industry** | I can shape organizational quality culture and prevention systems |

### ISTQB Alignment
- CTFL Chapter 5 — risk basics
- CTAL-TM (Test Manager) — strategy, risk management
- CT-AI — risk aspects of AI systems

---

## Dimension 4: 💬 INFLUENCE & IMPACT
**Core Question:** How much quality impact do you create through others?

### Sub-Areas

| Sub-Area | What It Covers |
|----------|---------------|
| **Collaboration** | Working with dev, PM, stakeholders effectively |
| **Communication** | Reporting, presenting, explaining quality status |
| **Ownership** | Taking responsibility for outcomes, not just tasks |
| **Leadership** | Mentoring, leading initiatives, building teams |
| **Domain Expertise** | Deep knowledge of your industry/product |

### Key Indicators by Stage

| Stage | Key Indicator |
|-------|---------------|
| **Starter** | I can communicate bug reports clearly to developers |
| **Practitioner** | I can explain testing status and blockers to my team |
| **Professional** | I can influence test priorities and negotiate with stakeholders |
| **Leader** | I can mentor others and lead quality initiatives beyond my team |
| **Industry** | I can shape how the organization (or industry) thinks about quality |

### ISTQB Alignment
- No direct certification — but domain-specific certs help (Agile, DevOps)

# PART 5: REGIONAL CONTEXT

> **Note:** Stage expectations vary by market maturity.

| Market | Automation | AI-Assisted | AI System Testing |
|--------|------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **US/Europe** | Assumed baseline | Current differentiator | Emerging |
| **India** | Key differentiator | Emerging advantage | Early |
| **Indonesia/SEA** | Differentiator | Early adopter advantage | Very early |

**What this means:**
- An Indonesian "Professional" in automation ≈ US "Practitioner" level
- This is NOT a judgment — it reflects market maturity
- The framework measures YOUR growth, not cross-regional comparison
- Early adoption of AI skills in Indonesia = competitive advantage
